The FSM structure is the following:

The execution begins in the state initial.

From initial, outputstreaminit moves it to outputstreamcreated.

When the monitor is in outputstreamcreated, write moves it to writing, flush moves it to flushed, and close moves it to closed.

When the monitor is in writing, write keeps it in writing, flush moves it to flushed, and close moves it to closed.

When the monitor is in flushed, flush keeps it in flushed, write moves it to writing, tobytearray keeps it in flushed, tostring keeps it in flushed, and close moves it to closed.

When the monitor is in closed, tobytearray keeps it in closed and tostring keeps it in closed.

The behavior evolves through the occurrence of these events: outputstreaminit, write, flush, close, tobytearray, tostring. The property describes a protocol that must be respected; violating the expected transition behavior should trigger a failure.
